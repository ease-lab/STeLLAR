Why vhive-bench?

Multiple Platforms Supported

Worried about which serverless provider offers the best performance to cost ratio? vHive-bench is designed to work with open-source platforms (vHive) and popular commercial cloud solutions (AWS Lambda). 

Comprehensive Latency Analysis

Worried about potential sources of tail or median latency but not sure where to begin looking? vHive-bench focuses on 4 distinct research dimensions: (1) bursty request behavior, (2) function image fetch delay, (3) inter-function transfer payload and (4) inter-function transfer chain length.

Highly Customizable

Want to create your own experiments? vHive-bench allows researchers and developers to customize 10+ parameters, including: number of bursts and their sizes, inter-arrival time, allocated function memory, inter-function transfer payload and chain length, and many more.

Importance of serverless benchmarking

Serverless computing has seen rapid adoption because of its instant scalability, flexible billing model, and economies of scale. In serverless, developers structure their applications as a collection of functions, sporadically invoked by various events like clicks. The high variability in function image sizes, invocation inter-arrival times,and request burst sizes motivates vendors to scrutinize their infrastructure to ensure a seamless user experience across all of their services.

To monitor serverless platform performance, identify pitfalls, and compare different providers, the public attention has turned to benchmarking, whereby measurement functions are deployed to the cloud to gather insights regarding response latencies, transfer speeds, and vendor policies.

This work introduces our open-source framework for serverless performance evaluation, intending to enable researchers and developers to benchmark multiple cloud platforms.
